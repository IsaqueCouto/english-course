Lesson 10 — Past Simple: Irregular Verbs

You already know the regular past with -ed (work → worked). Now we learn irregular verbs. These do NOT use -ed — the past is a new word you learn. The good news: the past form is the same for everyone (I, you, he, she, we, they). Negatives still use didn't + base verb, just like before.

Key language

Irregular verbs — base form → past form:

Base Past
be was / were
go went
have had
do did
get got
make made
see saw
come came
take took
eat ate
give gave
say said
find found
buy bought

PT note: Like ir → fui or fazer → fiz in Portuguese, these English verbs have a special past form to learn. But in English the form does not change with the subject — it is went for I, you, he, she, we, they.

Remember the trap: after didn't (and in questions with did), the verb goes back to the BASE form. I went → I didn't go. / Did you go? (NOT "Did you went?") Em português: depois de "didn't" e "did", o verbo volta à forma base.

(b) Write past sentences from the prompts

Use the irregular past. Example: Prompt: I / go / to work yesterday. → I went to work yesterday.

  1. She / make / a cake. → __________________________________________
  2. We / see / a good film. → __________________________________________
  3. They / come / home late. → __________________________________________
  4. He / get / a new job. → __________________________________________
  5. I / buy / some bread. → __________________________________________

(c) Fill the gap with the correct irregular past

Word bank: had — went — ate — gave — found

Example: Yesterday I went to the shop.

  1. She __________ a sandwich for lunch.
  2. We __________ a nice day at the park.
  3. He __________ me a present.
  4. I __________ my keys under the table.

(d) The "didn't" trap — make these sentences negative

The base verb comes back. Example: I went to the party. → I didn't go to the party.

  1. She had breakfast. → __________________________________________
  2. We saw the film. → __________________________________________
  3. He took the bus. → __________________________________________

(e) Error correction — fix the mistake and rewrite the sentence

Example: She goed home. → She went home.

  1. He didn't went to work. → __________________________________________
  2. I buyed a coffee. → __________________________________________
  3. Did you saw Maria? → __________________________________________
  4. They was at the office. → __________________________________________

(f) Read the story and answer in writing

Yesterday was a busy day for Carlos. He got up early and had a big breakfast. He went to the office by bus. At lunch he ate a sandwich and saw his friend Ana. After work he bought some flowers and gave them to his sister. It was a good day.

Answer the questions in full sentences. Example: What did Carlos eat at lunch? → He ate a sandwich.

  1. How did Carlos go to the office? → __________________________________________
  2. Who did he see at lunch? → __________________________________________
  3. What did he buy after work? → __________________________________________
  4. Was it a good day or a bad day for Carlos? → __________________________________________

(g) Write about a memorable day in your past

Write 4–5 sentences. Use irregular verbs from the table. Finish each starter:

  1. Last year I went __________________________________________
  2. In the morning I had __________________________________________
  3. I saw __________________________________________
  4. I bought __________________________________________
  5. It was __________________________________________

Homework

  1. Finish any sentences in (g) you did not complete.
  2. Write 4 short sentences using four DIFFERENT irregular verbs from the table:
    • two positive sentences (e.g. I made dinner.)
    • two negative sentences with didn't + base verb (e.g. I didn't make dinner.)

Remember: in the negative sentences the verb goes back to the base form (no irregular past after didn't).

Lesson 5 — Listening for Key Words

In IELTS Listening you usually hear something only once, and you must catch the key facts — names, numbers, times, dates, and places.

You do not need to understand every word. Just listen for the important ones — the key words (palavras-chave).

Today your teacher will read each text aloud twice, slowly. First time: just listen. Second time: write what you catch. You only listen and write — relax and enjoy it.

Listen and write

Your teacher tells you what to listen for, then reads. Write your answer in the blank.

Example (already done for you)

You hear: "My name is Ana. My number is three-three-one, two-zero-zero-six."

Listen for Write
Name Ana
Phone number 331 2006

Homework

1. At-home listening. Follow the listening practice in ielts/accent-listening.md. Listen for the key facts only — names, numbers, times, places. Do not worry about every word.

2. Find the key words (read silently, then write). Read this short text on your own and write the key facts in the table.

"The bus to the city leaves at quarter past seven in the morning. The bus stop is on King Street, number nine. The ticket costs four dollars."

Listen for Write

Street __________________
Stop number __________________
Ticket price __________________

Lesson 11 — Getting Around: Transport & Directions

In a new city you need to catch a bus or train, buy a ticket, and follow directions on the street. In this lesson you will learn the words for transport and directions, listen and write down times and numbers from short announcements, and write simple directions of your own. Your teacher reads everything aloud — your job is to read, listen, and write.

Key language

Transport

  • bus — ônibus (BR) / autocarro (PT)
  • train — trem (BR) / comboio (PT)
  • station — estação
  • stop (bus stop) — parada / paragem
  • ticket — bilhete / passagem
  • single / return — só ida / ida e volta
  • platform — plataforma / linha
  • timetable (schedule) — horário
  • on foot — a pé
  • get on / get off — subir (entrar) / descer (sair)

Directions

  • turn left / turn right — virar à esquerda / à direita
  • go straight — siga em frente
  • next to — ao lado de
  • on the corner — na esquina
  • opposite — em frente a / do lado oposto
  • traffic light — semáforo

Remember (prepositions of place): in, on, at, near, behind, between, in front of.

Practice

(a) Match the word to its meaning

Write the correct word from the box next to each meaning.

Word bank: station · ticket · platform · on foot · timetable · get off

Example: a place where trains stop and you get on or off → station

  1. the small paper you buy to travel → __________
  2. the part of the station where you wait for your train → __________
  3. a list of the times the bus or train leaves → __________
  4. walking, not by bus or car → __________
  5. to leave the bus or train → __________

(b) Listen & Write

Your teacher reads short announcements. Write the time, the platform or stop number, and the destination (where it goes) in the grid.

(c) Read & find

Read these directions. Then answer the questions in writing.

Start at the station. Go straight for two minutes. Turn left at the traffic light. The bank is on the corner. Walk past the bank and the café is next to it. The library is opposite the café, on the other side of the street.

Example question: What is on the corner after you turn left? → the bank

  1. Where do you turn left? → __________
  2. What is next to the bank? → __________
  3. Where is the library? → __________
  4. Do you travel by bus or on foot in these directions? → __________

(d) Find and fix the mistake

Each sentence has one mistake. Cross it out and write the correct word above it.

Example: I buy a ticket at the platform → station.

  1. Turn left in the traffic light.
  2. The shop is next the bank.
  3. I get off the train to start my trip. (Should I get on or get off to start?)
  4. The library is opposite to the café.

(e) Write your own directions (4–5 sentences)

Write directions from the station to the library. Use the sentence starters and the word bank.

Word bank: go straight · turn left · turn right · on the corner · next to · opposite · traffic light · on foot

Sentence starters:

  1. Start at the __________ .
  2. Go straight until __________ .
  3. Turn __________ at __________ .
  4. The library is __________ .
  5. You can get there __________ .

Homework

  1. Finish the error-correction exercise (d) and the directions-writing task (e) if you did not finish in class.
  2. Write a short note (3–4 sentences) telling a friend how to get from the station to a supermarket. Use turn left/right, go straight, and on the corner.
  3. Listen to ielts/accent-listening.md and write down any three transport or direction words you hear.

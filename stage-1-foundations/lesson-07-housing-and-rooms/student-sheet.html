Lesson 7 — Housing: Renting a Place

When you move to a new country, one of the first things you need is a place to live. In this lesson you will learn the words people use when they rent a home, read a real-style rental advertisement, and write your own sentences and questions about a place to live. You already know there is / there are and small words like in, near, next to, with — you will use them again here.

Key language

English Portuguese (short gloss)
apartment / flat apartamento (the two words mean the same)
house casa
room quarto / cômodo
rent (noun) o aluguel — the money you pay each month
rent (verb) alugar — to pay to use a place
landlord o senhorio — the person who owns the place
tenant o inquilino — the person who pays and lives there
deposit o depósito / caução — money you pay first and get back later
furnished / unfurnished mobiliado / sem mobília
utilities (water, electricity) serviços (água, eletricidade)
bills as contas — what you pay for utilities
lease / contract o contrato — the paper you sign to rent
neighbour o vizinho / a vizinha

Practice

(a) Fill in the word

Write the correct word in each gap. Use the word bank. There is an example.

Word bank: rent · landlord · furnished · deposit · bills · tenant

Example: The money I pay every month is the rent.

  1. The place already has a bed, a table, and chairs, so it is __________ .
  2. The person who owns the apartment is the __________ .
  3. Before I move in, I pay a __________ , and I get it back when I leave.
  4. Every month I also pay the __________ for water and electricity.
  5. I am the __________ , so I pay to live here.

(b) Read the rental advertisement

Read the advertisement. Then answer the 4 questions in writing.

Apartment for rent — city centre Nice apartment with two rooms, a kitchen, and a bathroom. The rent is 900 a month. The deposit is 900. The apartment is furnished. There is a bed and a sofa. Water is included, but electricity is not — you pay that bill. Near a bus stop and a supermarket. Friendly neighbours.

Example question: How many rooms are in the apartment? Example answer: There are two rooms.

  1. How much is the rent each month?
  1. Is the apartment furnished or unfurnished?
  1. Which utility do you pay for yourself?
  1. What is near the apartment?

(c) Write questions for the landlord

Imagine you are interested in a place. Write 4 questions to ask the landlord. Use the sentence starters. There is an example.

Example: How much is the rent?

  1. How much is the __________ ?
  2. Is the apartment __________ or unfurnished?
  3. Are the bills (water, electricity) __________ ?
  4. Can I see the __________ before I sign?

(d) Find and fix the mistakes

Each sentence has one mistake. Write the sentence correctly. There is an example.

Example: There is two rooms. → There are two rooms.

  1. I want rent a small apartment. → __________________________________
  2. How much the rent is? → __________________________________
  3. The landlord live next to me. → __________________________________
  4. There are a big kitchen in the house. → __________________________________

(e) Write about the place you want

Write 4–5 sentences about the kind of place you want to rent. Use the sentence starters. There is an example.

Example: I want to rent a small apartment near the city centre.

  • I want to rent a __________ .
  • There is / There are __________ .
  • The rent is __________ .
  • I want a furnished / unfurnished __________ .
  • It is near __________ .

Homework

  1. Look at a drawing of a place (or draw a simple one). Label it in English using 6 words from the Key language box.
  2. Write 4 sentences about the place where you live now, or a place you would like to live. Use there is / there are and the words near, with, next to.

Example: There are two rooms. There is a small kitchen. My apartment is near a bus stop. The rent is not too high.

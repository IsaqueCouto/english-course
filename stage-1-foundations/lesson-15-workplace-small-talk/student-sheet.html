Lesson 15 — Workplace Small-Talk and Politeness

At a new job, small kind words matter: a friendly greeting, a polite request, a short message to a coworker. In this lesson you will read and write polite workplace English. A big idea to remember: could and would are softer and more polite than can and will.

Key language

  • How are you? / How's it going? — Como vai? / Tudo bem?
  • Did you have a good weekend? — Teve um bom fim de semana?
  • Could you…? / Would you mind…? (polite requests) — Você poderia…? / Você se importaria de…?
  • Can I help you? — Posso ajudar?
  • Excuse me. — Com licença / Desculpe.
  • I'm sorry, I'm late. — Desculpe, estou atrasado(a).
  • Thank you. / No problem. — Obrigado(a). / Sem problema.
  • Email words: Hi [name], / Thanks, / Best, — Oi [nome], / Obrigado(a), / Atenciosamente,

Tip: Could you…, please? sounds kinder than Can you…? at work.

Practice

(a) Match the small-talk question to a good answer

Draw a line from each question to a suitable answer.

Example: "How are you?" → "I'm fine, thanks." ✅

Question Answer
1. How's it going? a. Yes, it was great. I rested.
2. Did you have a good weekend? b. No problem!
3. Can I help you? c. Pretty good, thanks.
4. Thank you so much. d. Yes, please. Could you help me with this?

(b) Make the blunt request polite

Rewrite each blunt order as a polite request. Use Could you…, please?

Example: Give me the report. → Could you give me the report, please? ✅

(c) Put the short message in the correct order

Number the lines 1–5 so the message makes sense.

Example (a different message, already ordered):

  1. Hi Sofia,
  2. I can't find the keys.
  3. Could you help me, please?
  4. Thanks,
  5. Lucas ✅

Now order this message:

___ Thanks, ___ Hi Marco, ___ Could you check it for me, please? ___ I think there is a problem with the printer. ___ Ana

(e) Read the email and answer in writing

Read this email from your manager.

Hi Maria,

Thanks for your good work this week. Could you send me the sales report before Friday, please? Diego is new on the team — would you mind helping him on Monday morning? No problem if you have questions; just ask me.

Best, Carlos

(f) Write your own short polite message (4–5 lines)

Write a short message to a coworker. Choose ONE: ask for help, or say you will be late. Use the frame below.

Frame:

  • Greeting: Hi ____ ,
  • Reason: I ____ . (why you are writing)
  • Request: Could you ____ , please? or Would you mind ____ ?
  • Thanks: Thanks,
  • Sign-off (your name): ____

Sentence starters to help you:

  • Hi Ana,
  • I can't find the new files. / I think the bus is late this morning.
  • Could you send them to me, please? / Would you mind starting without me?
  • Thanks,
  • (your name)

Lesson 17 — Short Messages and Notes

Every day people send short messages: a text to a friend, a note left on the table, a quick email. In this lesson you will read a few of these and then write one of your own. A friendly note has three parts: a greeting (hello), a message (what you want to say), and a sign-off (goodbye). That's all you need.

Key language

Openings (say hello)

  • Hi… / Hey… / Hello… — olá / oi

Closings (say goodbye — the "sign-off")

  • Thanks — obrigado/a
  • See you — até logo
  • Take care — cuide-se
  • Best — abraço (fechamento amigável)

Useful message lines

  • Do you want to…? — você quer…? (inviting)
  • Sorry, I can't… — desculpe, não posso… (apologizing)
  • Can you…? / Could you…? — você pode…? (asking a favour — favour = um favor)
  • I'll be late. — vou chegar atrasado/a. (leaving information)
  • Don't forget… — não esqueça…
  • Let me know. — me avise.

Practice

Exercise (a) — Match the message to its purpose

Read each message and match it to its purpose: invite, apologize, or remind.

Example (solved): "Hey! Don't forget your umbrella — it's raining today." → remind

  1. "Hi Sofia, do you want to come for coffee on Sunday?" → ____________
  2. "Sorry, I can't come to the party. I have to work." → ____________
  3. "Hey, don't forget to bring the tickets tonight!" → ____________

Exercise (b) — Read the email and answer

Read this short informal email, then answer the questions in writing.

Hi Lucas,

Thanks for your message. I'm sorry, but I can't meet on Friday — I have a meeting at work. Can we meet on Saturday instead? I'm free in the afternoon. Let me know if that's okay.

Take care, Maria

  1. Who wrote the email? ____________________________
  2. Why can't Maria meet on Friday? ____________________________
  3. Which day does she suggest instead? ____________________________
  4. When is she free that day? ____________________________

Exercise (c) — Fill the gaps in the note

Complete the note with words from the word bank. Each word is used once.

Word bank: Hi · Can · See you · Thanks

Example (solved): ____ Pedro, → Hi Pedro,

____ (1) Ana,

I'll be a little late tonight. ____ (2) you start dinner without me? ____ (3) for understanding.

____ (4) soon, Diego

Exercise (d) — Correct the errors

Each line has one clear mistake. Find it and write the line correctly.

Example (solved): hi Carla, can you call me? → Hi Carla, can you call me? (The greeting needs a capital letter: hi → Hi.)

  1. Hey Rosa, do you want to to come for lunch? → ____________________________
  2. Sorry, i can't come tomorrow. → ____________________________
  3. Can you to bring the keys? → ____________________________
  4. Don't forget the milk thanks → ____________________________ (add the comma and a sign-off line)

Exercise (e) — Write your own note

Read this message, then write a short note (4–5 lines) replying to it.

Message you received: Hi! We're going to the cinema on Saturday at 7. Do you want to come?

Use this frame. Fill each line.

Part Sentence starter
Greeting Hi __________,
Message Thanks for the invite! I'd love to / I'm sorry, but __________
Request / info Can you __________? / I'll __________
Sign-off See you, / Thanks,
(your name)

Example (solved — yes reply): Hi Bruno, Thanks for the invite! I'd love to come. Can you save me a seat? See you Saturday, Marta

Homework

Part 1 — Read and answer in writing.

Hi Tomás,

I'll be late on Thursday — my bus is always slow. Can you feed the cat for me? Don't forget the back door key is under the plant.

Thanks a lot, Elena

  1. What favour does Elena ask for? ____________________________
  2. Where is the key? ____________________________

Part 2 — Write a note. Write a short informal note (4–5 lines) inviting a friend to do something with you (a walk, a coffee, a film — you choose). Use the frame: greeting → message → request/info → sign-off. Underline your greeting and your sign-off so you can see all three parts are there.

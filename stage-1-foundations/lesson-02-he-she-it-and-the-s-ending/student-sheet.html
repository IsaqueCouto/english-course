Lesson 2 — He / She / It and the -s Ending

In Lesson 1 you wrote sentences with I, you, we, they. Now you will write about one other person: he, she, or it. The big new idea is small: with he / she / it, the verb almost always gets an -s. This sheet shows you the rules, then you write.

Nesta lição você escreve sobre outra pessoa (he / she / it). Com he, she, it, o verbo quase sempre ganha -s.

Key language

Rule 1 — Most verbs: add -s. work → works, eat → eats, live → lives, read → reads, drink → drinks. A maioria dos verbos só ganha -s.

Rule 2 — Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, or -o: add -es. watch → watches, finish → finishes, go → goes. Verbos terminados em -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o ganham -es.

Rule 3 — Consonant + y: change y → ies. study → studies, try → tries. (But play → plays, because there is a vowel before the y.) Consoante + y vira -ies. Mas se tem vogal antes do y, só ganha -s (play → plays).

Irregular verbs (just learn these three): have → has, go → goes, do → does. Três verbos irregulares: have → has, go → goes, do → does.

Negative — doesn't + base verb. After doesn't, the verb goes back to its base form (no -s). She works. → She doesn't work. Depois de doesn't, o verbo volta à forma base, sem -s.

Remember: don't = I / you / we / they. doesn't = he / she / it. Frequency adverbs (always, usually, often, sometimes, never) still go before the verb: He always works late. don't é para I/you/we/they; doesn't é para he/she/it. Os advérbios de frequência ficam antes do verbo.

Don't forget the -s! Não esqueça o -s!

Each sentence has one clear mistake. Rewrite it correctly. Cada frase tem um erro claro. Reescreva certo.

Remember: after doesn't, use the base verb (no -s). Depois de doesn't, use o verbo base, sem -s.

Example: She watches TV in the morning. → She doesn't watch TV in the morning. (watch, not watches)

(f) Read and answer in writing

Read about Maria, then answer the questions in full sentences. Leia e responda por escrito, em frases completas.

Maria lives in a small city. She works in a school as a teacher. Every morning she gets up at six and has coffee before work. She doesn't drive — she goes to work by bus. In the evening she studies for an English exam.

  1. Where does Maria work? ____________________
  2. What does she have in the morning? ____________________
  3. How does she go to work? ____________________
  4. What does she do in the evening? ____________________

(g) Write about a family member or friend (~4–5 sentences)

Choose one person (he or she). Use the frame and starters below. Try to use one doesn't sentence and one frequency adverb (always / usually / sometimes / never). Escreva 4–5 frases sobre uma pessoa, usando o esquema. Tente usar um doesn't e um advérbio de frequência.

Frame and starters:

  • My ____________ (brother / sister / friend / mother) ____________ (his/her name).
  • He / She works (or studies) ____________________.
  • Every day he / she ____________________.
  • He / She usually ____________________.
  • He / She doesn't ____________________.

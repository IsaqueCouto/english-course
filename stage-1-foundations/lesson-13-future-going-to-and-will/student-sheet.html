Lesson 13 — The Future: "going to" and "will"

In this lesson you will write about the future. There are two ways to talk about the future in English. You use be going to for plans you already decided, and will for decisions you make right now, for predictions, and for offers. Read the rules, then write.

Key language

1. be going to + base verb — for plans and intentions (you already decided).

  • I am going to study tonight.
  • She is going to work tomorrow.
  • They are going to move next year.
  • Negative: She isn't going to work Sunday. / We aren't going to travel.
  • PT: going to = um plano já decidido. Use am / is / are + going to + verbo base.

2. will + base verb — for decisions made now, predictions, and offers.

  • It will rain tomorrow. (prediction)
  • I**'ll** help you. (offer — 'll = will)
  • I will call you soon. (decision now)
  • Negative: I won't be late. (won't = will not)
  • PT: will = decisão na hora, previsão, ou oferta. Use will + verbo base (igual para todos).

Two small traps (be careful!):

  • After will, use the base verb with no "to": ✅ I will help / ❌ I will to help. (PT: nada de to depois de will.)
  • going to needs the verb be: ✅ She is going to work / ❌ She going to work. (PT: going to precisa de am/is/are.)

Future time markers: tomorrow, next week, next year, soon, tonight.

Practice

(a) Write "going to" sentences

Use am / is / are + going to + base verb. Add a time marker from the word bank.

Word bank (time markers): tonight · tomorrow · next week · next year · soon

Example: (I / cook dinner) → I am going to cook dinner tonight.

  1. (She / clean the house) → ____________________________________
  2. (We / visit my family) → ____________________________________
  3. (They / buy a car) → ____________________________________
  4. (He / start a new job) → ____________________________________
  5. (I / learn English) → ____________________________________

(b) Choose: going to or will?

Read each situation. Write the correct form. Plan already decided → going to. Decision now, prediction, or offer → will.

Example: Look at those dark clouds. → It will rain. (prediction)

  1. We bought the tickets. We ______________ (going to / will) travel next week. ( ______ plan or prediction? )
  2. Your bag looks heavy. I ______________ (going to / will) help you. ( ______ offer? )
  3. She has a plan: she ______________ (going to / will) study medicine next year. ( ______ )
  4. I think the test ______________ (going to / will) be easy. ( ______ )
  5. The phone is ringing. Don't worry, I ______________ (going to / will) answer it. ( ______ )

(c) Make it negative

Rewrite each sentence in the negative. Use isn't/aren't going to or won't.

Example: She is going to work on Sunday. → She isn't going to work on Sunday.

  3. He is going to buy a new phone. → ____________________________________
  4. It will rain tomorrow. → ____________________________________

Write 4–5 sentences about your own future. Write four plans for next week with going to, and one prediction with will. Use the sentence starters.

Homework

  1. Rewrite these four sentences in the negative:
    • I will call you tonight.
    • We are going to study tomorrow.
    • She is going to cook dinner.
    • It will be hot next week.

Lesson 18 — Stage 1 Review

This is the last lesson of Stage 1. Today you bring everything together. You will write present, past, and future sentences, read a short text, and write a short paragraph about yourself. Each part starts with an example, so you always see the pattern first. Take your time and write clearly.

Mixed Review

(a) Present simple

Use the present simple for routines and facts. Remember: he / she / it takes -s.

Example: (I / work / in a shop) → I work in a shop.

  1. (They / live / in a small town) → ____________________________________
  2. (We / start / work at eight) → ____________________________________
  3. (I / drink / coffee every morning) → ____________________________________

(b) He / she / it and the -s ending

Add the correct verb. Remember the -s for he, she, and it.

Example: My sister ______ (work) in a hospital. → My sister works in a hospital.

  1. He ______ (live) near the station.
  2. She ______ (study) English every day.
  3. The bus ______ (stop) here at nine.

(c) Questions

Make a question. Use Do / Does or the question word given.

Example: (she / work / on Sundays?) → Does she work on Sundays?

  1. (you / live / near here?) → ____________________________________
  2. (Where / he / work?) → ____________________________________
  3. (What time / the shop / open?) → ____________________________________

(d) There is / There are

Use there is for one thing and there are for more than one.

Example: (two bedrooms / in the flat) → There are two bedrooms in the flat.

  1. (a big kitchen / in the house) → ____________________________________
  2. (three buses / every hour) → ____________________________________
  3. (a supermarket / near the station) → ____________________________________

(e) Present continuous (actions now)

Use am / is / are + verb-ing for what is happening now.

Example: (I / read / a book / now) → I am reading a book now.

  1. (She / cook / dinner / right now) → ____________________________________
  2. (They / wait / for the bus) → ____________________________________
  3. (I / write / a message) → ____________________________________

(f) Past simple — regular verbs

Past simple regular verbs usually end in -ed.

Example: Yesterday I (work) late. → Yesterday I worked late.

  1. Last week we (visit) my family. → ____________________________________
  2. She (clean) the house on Saturday. → ____________________________________
  3. They (start) a new job last month. → ____________________________________

(g) Past simple — irregular verbs

These verbs do not take -ed. Use the word bank.

Word bank: went · had · saw · came · bought

Example: Yesterday I (go) to the market. → Yesterday I went to the market.

  1. Last night we (have) dinner with friends. → ____________________________________
  2. On Monday she (buy) a new coat. → ____________________________________
  3. He (come) home late yesterday. → ____________________________________

(h) Future — going to and will

Use going to for a plan you already decided. Use will for a decision now, a prediction, or an offer.

Example: (plan) Next week I ______ start a new class. → Next week I am going to start a new class.

  1. (plan) Tomorrow she ______ visit her friend. → ____________________________________
  2. (prediction) I think it ______ rain later. → ____________________________________
  3. (offer) Your bag is heavy. I ______ help you. → ____________________________________

Reading

Read the text. Then answer the questions in full sentences.

Carlos is from Mexico. He lives in a small flat in the city. There is one bedroom and a small kitchen. Every morning he takes the bus to work, and he starts at nine o'clock. Last week he had a busy time: he worked late on three days and he bought a new jacket for the cold weather. Next month he is going to start an English class, because he thinks it will help him at work.

  1. Where is Carlos from? → ____________________________________
  2. How does he go to work every morning? → ____________________________________
  3. What did he do last week? (write two things) → ____________________________________
  4. What is he going to do next month, and why? → ____________________________________

Write about yourself

Write a short paragraph of about six sentences about you. Use present, past, and future together. Follow the frame. Write true sentences about your own life.

Example: My name is Ana. I work in a shop and I start at nine o'clock. Right now I am studying English at home. Last week I visited my family and we had dinner together. Next month I am going to look for a new job. I think it will be a good year for me.

Frame — write one or two sentences for each part:

  1. Who you are (present simple): My name is ______ . I ______ ...
  2. Your routine now (present simple / present continuous): Every day I ______ ... / Right now I am ______ ...
  3. Something you did last week (past simple): Last week I ______ ...
  4. Your plans for the future (going to / will): Next month I am going to ______ ... / I think it will ______ ...

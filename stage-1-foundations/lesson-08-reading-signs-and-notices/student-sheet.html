Lesson 8 — Reading Signs and Notices

Everywhere you go, there are signs and notices — on doors, on walls, on machines, in shops. (signs and notices = placas e avisos) They are short, but they tell you something important: when a shop is open, where you can park, which door is for workers.

The big idea today: you do not need to understand every word. Read the whole sign, look at where it is, and guess the meaning. This is exactly the first thing the IELTS reading test asks you to do.

Here are six short signs. Read each one. Don't worry about new words — guess from the picture in your head of where you would see it.

Sign 1

OPEN Monday–Friday: 9:00–18:00 Saturday: 10:00–14:00 Sunday: CLOSED

Sign 2

NO PARKING Garage in use 24 hours

Sign 3

OUT OF ORDER Please use the next machine

Sign 4

STAFF ONLY

Sign 5

PUSH

Sign 6

NO SMOKING This is a non-smoking building

Task A — Match each sign to its meaning

Write the number of the sign next to the meaning. The first one is done for you as an example.

Meaning Sign number
This machine is broken; do not use it. 3 (example)
Do not leave your car here. ____
Only workers can go in here, not customers. ____
You cannot smoke anywhere in this building. ____
This shop is closed on Sunday. ____
Move the door away from you to go in. ____

Task B — True / False / Not Given

This is a famous IELTS question type. You read a short notice, then you read some statements. For each statement you write one of three answers:

  • True — the notice says this. It agrees with the notice.
  • False — the notice says the opposite. It disagrees with the notice.
  • Not Given — the notice does not tell us. There is no information about it. (Not Given = não informado)

The tricky part: False is not the same as Not Given. False means the notice says something different. Not Given means the notice says nothing at all about it.

Read this notice:

NOTICE TO ALL STAFF The office will close early at 15:00 this Friday for cleaning. Please switch off your computer before you leave. The office will open as normal on Monday.

Example: The office will close early on Friday. → True (the notice says this)

Now write True, False, or Not Given for each:

  1. The office will close at 17:00 on Friday. → ____________
  2. Staff must turn off their computers before leaving. → ____________
  3. The cleaning will take two hours. → ____________
  4. The office is open as usual on Monday. → ____________
  5. All staff will get the afternoon free as paid time. → ____________

(Hint: for number 3 and number 5, ask yourself — does the notice actually tell me this? If not, it is Not Given.)

Task C — Where would you see this sign?

Read each sign and write the place where you would see it. The first one is done for you.

Sign: OUT OF ORDER Place: on a broken machine, for example a coffee machine, a lift, or an ATM (example)

  1. Sign: STAFF ONLY Place: ____________________________

  2. Sign: NO PARKING — Garage in use Place: ____________________________

  3. Sign: OPEN — Monday–Friday 9:00–18:00 Place: ____________________________

  4. Sign: PUSH Place: ____________________________

Task D — Read the longer notice and answer the questions

This is like a real IELTS Section 1 reading. Read the notice slowly. Remember: read for meaning, and guess any new words from the rest of the text.

LAUNDRY ROOM RULES The laundry room is open every day from 7:00 to 22:00. Each washing machine costs 2 coins per wash. Please do not leave your clothes in the machine after it finishes — other people are waiting. If a machine is not working, write a note and put it on the door of the building manager, Mr. Lopez.

Answer in writing, in full short sentences where you can:

  1. What time does the laundry room close? → ____________________________

  2. How much does one wash cost? → ____________________________

  3. What should you do with your clothes when the machine finishes? → ____________________________

  4. Who do you tell if a machine is broken? → ____________________________

Homework

  1. Find or remember three signs from real life — a shop, a door, a machine, or a notice. Write each sign out exactly, then write one sentence for each: what does it mean, and where would you see it?

    Example:

    Sign: CLOSED FOR LUNCH Meaning: The shop is shut for a short time at lunch. You would see it on a shop door.

  2. Read the Laundry Room Rules notice again (Task D). Write two new True / False / Not Given statements of your own about it — and write the correct answer next to each. Try to make at least one of them a Not Given!

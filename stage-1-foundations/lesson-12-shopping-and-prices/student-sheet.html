Lesson 12 — Shopping, Money, and Prices

In this lesson you learn the words for shopping. You will read prices, sizes, and a receipt, and you will write simple shopping questions and sentences. Everything here is reading and writing. You do not need to speak.

Key language

English Portuguese gloss
shop / store loja
price preço
cheap barato
expensive caro
size — small / medium / large tamanho — pequeno / médio / grande
cash dinheiro (em espécie)
card cartão
change troco
receipt recibo / nota
discount / sale desconto / promoção
How much is…? / How much are…? Quanto custa…? / Quanto custam…?
I'd like… Eu queria… / Gostaria de…
Can I pay by card? Posso pagar com cartão?

Remember: use is for one thing, and are for two or more things. We write the money symbol before the number: **5 * *(not5).

Practice

(a) Match the price to the item / write the price in words

Example (solved): $2 — two dollars — a coffee

Now match each price to the right item, then write the number in words. (Word bank: three, five, ten)

  1. $5 — __________ — a sandwich
  2. $3 — __________ — an apple
  3. $10 — __________ — a small bag

(b) Complete the shopping question with is or are

Example (solved): How much is the coffee? (one coffee → is)

Complete each question. Hint: look for the plural -s.

  1. How much ______ the shoes? (shoes = more than one)
  2. How much ______ the milk?
  3. How much ______ the bananas?
  4. How much ______ the bag?

(c) Read the receipt and answer in writing

Read this receipt from a small store.

GREEN STREET STORE
RECEIPT
-----------------------
Bread          $2
Milk           $3
Apples (x4)    $4
Bag            $1
-----------------------
TOTAL          $10
Paid by card
Discount: $0

Example (solved): Q: How much is the bread? A: The bread is $2.

Now answer in writing:

  1. How much is the milk? __________________________
  2. What is the total? __________________________
  3. Which is cheaper, the bread or the milk? __________________________
  4. Did the customer pay by cash or by card? __________________________

(d) Error correction — fix the mistake

Example (solved): How much is the shoes? → How much are the shoes? (shoes = plural → are)

Rewrite each sentence correctly:

  1. How much are the milk? → __________________________
  2. I like a medium coffee, please. → __________________________ (use the polite shopping form)
  3. The sandwich is cheap. It is only 5$. → __________________________ (money symbol)
  4. How much is the bananas? → __________________________

(e) Write a short shopping dialogue (writing only)

You are the customer. Write the customer's lines. The shop worker's lines are already written for you. Use the sentence starters.

Example (solved customer line): Worker: Hello! Can I help you? Customer (you): Yes, I'd like a small bag, please.

Now write your lines:

Worker: Hello! Can I help you? Customer (you): Yes, I'd like _______________________ .

Worker: Of course. It is $4. Customer (you): How much _______________________ the apples? (is or are?)

Worker: The apples are $3. Customer (you): Can I pay _______________________ ?

Worker: Yes, card is fine. Here is your receipt. Customer (you): Thank you. _______________________ . (write a polite ending)

Homework

Part 1 — Write four shopping questions. Write two with is and two with are. Starters:

  • How much is the __________ ?
  • How much is the __________ ?
  • How much are the __________ ?
  • How much are the __________ ?

Part 2 — Write a short note (three lines) about a shopping trip. Use the starters:

  • I'd like __________ .
  • The __________ is cheap / expensive.
  • I paid by cash / card.

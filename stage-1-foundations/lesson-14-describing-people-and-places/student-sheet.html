Lesson 14 — Describing People and Places

In this lesson you will learn words to describe people and places, and where to put them in a sentence. Remember: in English the describing word (the adjective) usually comes before the noun — "a big house" — not after it like in Portuguese ("uma casa grande").

Key language

Appearance (how someone looks):

  • tall (alto), short (baixo), young (jovem), old (velho / mais velho), thin (magro)

Personality (what someone is like):

  • kind (gentil), friendly (simpático), funny (engraçado), serious (sério), quiet (quieto / calado)

Places (what a place is like):

  • big (grande), small (pequeno), clean (limpo), busy (movimentado), beautiful (bonito), modern (moderno), old (antigo), quiet (tranquilo)

Where the adjective goes:

  • Before a noun: a tall man — a clean room
  • After "be": He is tall. — The room is clean.

Two or three adjectives together (light pattern): opinion + size + age, for example "a nice small old house". You only need to recognize this for now.

Tip: English flips the Portuguese order. "uma casa grande" becomes "a big house".

Practice

(a) Put the adjective in the right place

Rewrite each sentence with the adjective in the correct place.

Example: She lives in a house. (big) → She lives in a big house.

  1. He is a man. (tall)
  2. They have a car. (modern)
  3. I know a teacher. (friendly)
  4. We visited a city. (beautiful)

(b) Match the opposites

Draw a line or write the matching pair.

Example: tall — short

  1. big — ______
  2. young — ______
  3. busy — ______
  4. clean — ______

Word bank: quiet, small, old, dirty

(c) Choose the right adjective

Read each description and choose the best adjective from the word bank. Write it in the gap.

Word bank: funny, busy, tall, kind

Example: My uncle is very high — his head is near the door. He is tall.

  1. The street has many cars and many people. The street is ______.
  2. My friend always helps me and gives nice gifts. She is ______.
  3. He tells jokes and makes everyone laugh. He is ______.

(d) Correct the mistakes

Each sentence has one clear mistake. Write the correct sentence.

Example: I live in a apartment small. → I live in a small apartment.

  1. She is a person kind. → ______
  2. They have a house big. → ______
  3. He is a man serious. → ______
  4. We saw two olds buildings. → ______

(e) Read and answer in writing

Read this description, then write your answers in full sentences.

My friend Carlos is from Mexico. He is short and thin, and he is very friendly. He works in a small office in the city centre. The office is old but clean. After work, he walks in a beautiful quiet park near his home.

Example question: Where is Carlos from? → Carlos is from Mexico.

  1. Is Carlos tall or short? ______
  2. What is his personality like? ______
  3. What is the office like? (two adjectives) ______
  4. What is the park like? ______

(f) Write about a person and a place

Write 4–5 sentences. Describe a person you know, then a place you like. Use at least four adjectives from this lesson. Use the sentence starters.

Example: My sister is young and kind. She is also funny.

  1. My ______ is ______ .
  2. He / She is also ______ .
  3. A place I like is ______ .
  4. It is ______ and ______ .
  5. I like this place because it is ______ .

Homework

  1. Write 4–5 sentences describing a family member or a friend. Use at least four adjectives from this lesson.
  2. Write 2–3 sentences describing a place near where you live (for example a street, a park, or a shop).
  3. Underline every adjective you used. Check that each one is in the right place: before the noun ("a big house") or after "be" ("The house is big").
